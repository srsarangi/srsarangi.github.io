                                                               WELCOME to the homepage of CSL 211 course !!

 
Computer Architecture is the science and art of selecting and interconnecting hardware components to create computers that meet  functional, performance and cost goals.

                         


The 'Computer Architecture' course is intended to teach undergraduate students in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science the basics of computer architectures.

Credits :  5   [3-1-2] (L-T-P)

Prerequisites:

 CSL101/CSL102 , CSL201 & (EEL201 & EEP201) (concurrent registration in EEL201 & EEP201 is acceptable)

TextBook:

Computer Organization and Design, 4th Edition  The Hardware/Software Interface  "ARM EDITION"

By David A. Patterson  &  John L. Hennessy
